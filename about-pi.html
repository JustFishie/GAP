What is Pi?

Many centuries ago, people discovered the ratio between the circumference and the diameter of a circle is a constant. William Jones first named this constant π and then Leonhard Euler popularized it.

π = C/d of a circle. The value of this constant was thought to be rational but later proved to be irrational.

π = 3.14159265358979323846264338…

A Brief History of Pi

Starting in 2000 B.C.E the Babylonians used 3.125 and the Egyptians used (16/9)² or 3.1605. In the third century Archimedes used a 96 sided polygon to estimate π. In 1220 Leonardo de Pisa (Fibonacci) computed that π = 3.141818… Then in 1706 John Machin calculates π to 100 digits. 1959 arrives and François Genuys with his teammates calculated π to 16,167 digits. 36 years later, the Chudnovsky brothers calculated pi to 8 billion digits. Finally in 1997 Kamada and Takahasi calculate pi to 51.5 billion digits.
